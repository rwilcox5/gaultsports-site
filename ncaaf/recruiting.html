Recruiting Rankings

Unbiased College Football Team Recruiting Rankings

Do you feel like recruiting rankings are biased?

What if there was a source of unbiased ratings for high school prospects? Coaches judge players and if they do a poor job they get fired.

TripleLog rankings use data on scholarship offers to identify the best recruiting programs. At its simplest, teams that win recruiting battles over top programs gain points and programs that lose battles also lose points.

Our rankings are based on ELO ratings, but adjusted slightly to account for the fact that the information is not complete. As usual, each team starts with a rating of 1500. Every commitment is considered a victory for the chosen program and a defeat for every other school that made an offer.

Beating an FCS school is not the same as beating an SEC school, so we want to more heavily reward good victories. Each pair of winner and loser is treated like a separate competition. An expected probabilty is computed and used to determine how many points the winning team gains and the losing team loses. The probability that the losing team could have won is multiplied by a fixed amount to determine the change.

If Alabama is competing with South Alabama then there may only be a 1% chance of South Alabama getting the recruit. So if Alabama we would only add 1% of some constant. However, if South Alabama wins the recruit then we would add 99% of that constant. Thus good teams beating bad teams doesn't change much, but a big upset really change the ratings.

While offer data is quite robust, some schools do not offer scholarships unless they really think they have a chance for a prospect. The top recruits could go to any school they choose, but not every school actually makes a formal offer that gets recorded. We don't want to reward programs for refusing to offer scholarships to players that are out of their league. So we add a small amount back for each loss. Thus the penalty for each loss is some sort of loss below replacement. Programs that offer everyone are not penalized simply for dreaming big, but they are still penalized for losing battles.
